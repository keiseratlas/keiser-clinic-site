Chiropractic Neurology

What exactly is a chiropractic neurologist? A chiropractic neurologist has had additional post graduate training in clinical neuroscience after their Doctor of Chiropractic degree. This training focuses on understanding how the nervous system works and how to optimize its function through non-pharmaceutical and non-surgical means.

At The Keiser Clinic, we combine advanced diagnostic technologies with the latest in clinical neuroscience to find what's driving your symptoms — and treat it at the source.

A Treatment Approach That Works

Comprehensive Neurological Examination

Our evaluation goes far beyond standard neurological testing. We assess vestibular function, oculomotor control, cerebellar timing, and autonomic responses to build a complete picture of how your nervous system is functioning.

Autonomic & Neurovascular Testing

Using tilt table testing and transcranial Doppler, we measure real-time blood flow to your brain and watch how your autonomic system responds to positional changes. This reveals the mechanism driving your symptoms.

Advanced Eye Movement Analysis

Your eyes are a window into brain function. We use video oculography and eye tracking to assess how your brain is processing information and identify areas of dysfunction that contribute to your symptoms.

Targeted Neurological Rehabilitation

Once we identify your specific mechanism, we create a personalized treatment plan using vestibular rehabilitation, oculomotor exercises, and neuroplasticity-based therapies to address the root cause.

Structural & Manual Therapies

When structural issues contribute to your symptoms, we incorporate chiropractic care, soft tissue work, and targeted exercises to address cervical instability, positional compression, and musculoskeletal contributors.
